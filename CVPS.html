Your Job Search Toolkit

Finding the right opportunity is the first step to success. Below are curated resources to help you search effectively, whether you're looking for a graduate role in the UK, a position in a specific sector, or an international opportunity.

Step 2: Prepare Your Story (CV)

Your CV is more than a document; it's your professional story. Use these resources to build a compelling CV that grabs an employer's attention.

  • CV Booster Pathway on MyCareer - A step-by-step online guide to writing a professional CV.
  • Book a CV Support Appointment - Get 1-to-1 feedback from a careers advisor.

Top CV Tips

  • Beat the Bots: Many large companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to scan CVs. Use standard headings (e.g., 'Work Experience', 'Education') and include keywords from the job description.
  • Quantify Your Achievements: Instead of "Improved team efficiency," write "Increased team productivity by 15% by introducing a new project management tool." Numbers show real impact.
  • Tailor Everything: A generic CV gets generic results. For every application, adjust your summary and highlighted skills to perfectly match the job you're applying for.

Step 4: Ace Your Interview

Proper preparation is the key to interview success. Use these tools to practice and build your confidence.

  • E-learning module "Interview Success" - Highly Recommended!
  • Sign up for Interviews Workshops - Practice in an interactive group setting.
  • Book an Ask the Adviser appointment - Run through a mock interview.

How to Make the Most of Workshops (like Workshop 2: Interview Skills)

  • Prepare Beforehand: Review the workshop topic. If it's for interviews, come with a specific job role in mind to practice with.
  • Participate Actively: Ask questions and take part in activities. The more you engage, the more you'll learn and build confidence.
  • Take Action After: Don't let the knowledge fade! Apply what you learned right away or book a follow-up 1-to-1 appointment to discuss further.
