International Student Careers Programme

As an international student at the University of Southampton, you can sign up for an enhanced programme of careers support, tailored to meet your needs. This includes five workshops which can be attended live or watched as recordings, a dedicated LinkedIn group, personalised appointments, regular international alumni careers panels, networking events and support from our Specialist Careers Consultant. For students planning to work in China after graduation, there will also be regular webinars from employers, delivered in Mandarin.

Note: This programme is specially designed for postgraduate students with some industry experience. We focus on building personalised and agile career strategies, like harnessing the temporary contractor market and using the right recruitment agencies.

If you're an undergraduate or don't have industry experience, this content is still highly relevant, and you are very welcome to join! You can also access the full range of support from Careers, Employability and Student Enterprise.

💼 Work While You Study

Part-time work and work experience can help you financially, build relevant experience, grow your network, and improve your professional English skills. Whether you're a postgraduate with existing industry experience or an undergraduate building your profile, there are many opportunities available.

Important: You must always comply with the conditions of your visa when taking on any paid work. Please check how many hours you can work on a students visa before you apply for roles.For more information, please use the visa team service.

🎓 Planning for After Graduation

It is likely that your main goal will be to find professional employment in your chosen field after graduation. Whether this is in the UK, back home or in a new country, you will need to be planning your career strategy alongside your studies. There are different types of employment that you could secure as a graduate, depending on your experience, sector, and skills.check our back to China page for advice

In the UK, many large companies recruit graduates onto schemes with an integrated training period (usually 1–2 years). These are attractive opportunities but are extremely competitive.

Key Features of Graduate Schemes:

  • Recruited up to 13 months before the start date (e.g., jobs advertised in August 2024 for a September 2025 start).
  • Have multiple application stages, often including online tests and assessment centres.
  • Usually with larger organisations hiring multiple candidates.

You can find vacancies on specialist graduate job boards, including:

  • Bright Network
  • Targetjobs
  • Gradcracker
  • Prospects

Remember: You need to start applying at the beginning of your final year. For Master's students, this means as soon as you arrive. This can be overwhelming, so access our support services early!

As soon as you have your university profile, you can book an appointment or access the international student careers programme on MyCareer. If unsure, email careers@southampton.ac.uk.

Many students, especially those on Postgraduate courses, already have significant work experience. The experienced hire market may be more suitable, offering roles and salaries that match your existing skill set. The International Student Careers Programme is designed to focus on this job market.

How to Prepare Before You Arrive:

  • Research the Market: Use the 'Jobs' section on Indeed and LinkedIn to see what jobs exist that match your skills and what the typical salaries are.
  • Focus on Keywords: Search using skills and keywords rather than specific job titles, which can vary widely in the UK.
  • Create Your LinkedIn Profile: If you don't have one, create it now. It is a valuable networking tool, research platform, and jobs board.
  • Use LinkedIn for Research: Look at people with similar qualifications. See where they work and the career path that got them there.

📋 Visas and Working in the UK

Visas and Working in the UK

Find general information on the Student Hub website. You can contact the Visa and Immigration Student Advice Service (VISAS) by email, live chat, and through visa advice appointments.

Self-Employment

International students must comply with visa regulations for self-employment. Please see the Enterprise section on our Work while you are studying page for more information.

English Language Support

The University provides term-time courses which cover presentation skills, everyday English, and academic writing.

National Insurance (NI) Number

You will need a National Insurance number to work in the UK. See the UK Government website to apply.

💡 Starting a Business?

introduce business idea programme

If you plan to start your own business or be self-employed, you must follow specific visa regulations.

For International Students:
This UKCISA blog provides detailed information on self-employment while on a Student visa. It is essential that you contact the University's Visa and Immigration Student Advice Service (VISAS) before starting any self-employment.

For International Graduates:
After graduation, certain visas may permit self-employment:

  • Innovator Founder visa: Requires a completely new business idea endorsed by an approved body.
  • Graduate visa: Usually allows self-employment for the duration of the visa.
